Descriptive Statistics

Mean

$$\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}$$

Weighted Mean

$$\bar{x}=\frac{\sum wx}{\sum w}$$

Range

$$\text{Range}=\max(x)-\min(x)$$

Sample Variance

$$s^2=\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}$$

Sample Standard Deviation

$$s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}$$

Population Variance

$$\sigma^2=\frac{\sum(x-\mu)^2}{N}$$

Population Standard Deviation

$$\sigma=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\mu)^2}{N}}$$

z‑score

$$z=\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}$$

Probability Basics

Classical Probability

$$P(E)=\frac{\text{favorable}}{\text{possible}}$$

Complement Rule

$$P(E')=1-P(E)$$

Addition Rule

$$P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)$$

Multiplication Rule

$$P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B\mid A)$$

Conditional Probability

$$P(A\mid B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}$$

Counting & Combinatorics

Factorial

$$n!=n(n-1)(n-2)\cdots1$$

Permutations

$$_nP_r=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$$

Combinations

$$_nC_r=\frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$$

Binomial Coefficient

$$\binom{n}{r}=_nC_r$$

Discrete Distributions

Binomial PMF

$$P(X=k)=\binom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k}$$

Binomial Mean

$$\mu=np$$

Binomial SD

$$\sigma=\sqrt{np(1-p)}$$

Normal Distribution

Standardization

$$z=\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}$$

Empirical Rule

68% within 1σ
95% within 2σ
99.7% within 3σ

Confidence Intervals

CI for Mean (σ Known)

$$\bar{x}\pm z_{\alpha/2}\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$

CI for Mean (σ Unknown)

$$\bar{x}\pm t_{\alpha/2}\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$$

CI for Proportion

$$\hat{p}\pm z_{\alpha/2}\sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}$$

1‑Population Tests

Mean (σ Known)

$$z=\frac{\bar{x}-\mu_0}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}}$$

Mean (σ Unknown)

$$t=\frac{\bar{x}-\mu_0}{s/\sqrt{n}}$$

Proportion

$$z=\frac{\hat{p}-p_0}{\sqrt{p_0(1-p_0)/n}}$$

2‑Population Tests

Difference of Means (σ Known)

$$z=\frac{(\bar{x}_1-\bar{x}_2)-(\mu_1-\mu_2)} {\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1}+\frac{\sigma_2^2}{n_2}}}$$

Difference of Means (σ Unknown)

$$t=\frac{(\bar{x}_1-\bar{x}_2)-(\mu_1-\mu_2)} {\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1}+\frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}}$$

Difference of Proportions

$$z=\frac{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2} {\sqrt{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})(\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2})}}$$

where \( \hat{p}=\frac{x_1+x_2}{n_1+n_2} \)

Sample Size

Sample Size for Mean

$$n=\left(\frac{z_{\alpha/2}\sigma}{E}\right)^2$$

Sample Size for Proportion

$$n=\frac{z_{\alpha/2}^2 p(1-p)}{E^2}$$

If no estimate for \(p\), use 0.5

Glossary of Symbols

Parameters

\(\mu\): population mean
\(\sigma\): population standard deviation
\(p\): population proportion
\(N\): population size

Statistics

\(\bar{x}\): sample mean
\(s\): sample standard deviation
\(\hat{p}\): sample proportion
\(n\): sample size

Test Notation

\(z_{\alpha/2}\), \(t_{\alpha/2}\): critical values
\(H_0, H_a\): null and alternative hypotheses

Distributions

\(X\): random variable
\(Z\): standard normal
“pooled” \( \hat{p} \): combined sample proportion
